My Favorite Movies

A collection of movies I love and recommend!

These are some of my favorite movies: ones I always find myself going back to. Some are cult classics, others are just fun to watch, but they all have something special. Whether it’s the storytelling, the visuals, or just the overall vibe, each of these films has left a lasting impression on me.

If you haven’t seen some of these yet, add them to your Letterboxd watchlist. There’s a good mix here: sci-fi, action, thrillers, and a few that are just impossible to categorize. Hopefully, you’ll find something new to love.

Akira (1988)

A visually stunning cyberpunk epic set in Neo-Tokyo, where a biker gang member unlocks dangerous psychic abilities that threaten to unravel the city.

Blade Runner (2049)

A breathtaking neo-noir sequel where a replicant detective uncovers a buried secret that could change the course of humanity.

Drive (2011)

A stylish, neon-soaked thriller about a quiet stunt driver who moonlights as a getaway driver and gets caught up in a deadly heist.

Escape from New York (1981)

In a dystopian future where Manhattan is a maximum-security prison, a rugged outlaw is sent in to rescue the President.

Ghost in the Shell (1995)

A philosophical cyberpunk classic following a cyborg detective questioning her identity while hunting a mysterious hacker.

Pulp Fiction (1994)

A genre-defying, darkly comedic crime film that weaves together multiple intertwining stories of hitmen, gangsters, and unexpected twists.

Robocop (1987)

A brutal and satirical sci-fi action film about a murdered cop reborn as a cybernetic enforcer in a crime-ridden future.

The Matrix (1999)

A mind-bending action thriller where a hacker discovers reality is a simulation and joins a rebellion against its machine overlords.

Cyber City OEDO 808 (1990)

A stylish, high-energy cyberpunk anime about convicted criminals forced to work as cyber-cops to reduce their sentences.
